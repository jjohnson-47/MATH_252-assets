WEEK 3: Integration Techniques

∫∫∫

Learning Objectives

  • Apply u-substitution to evaluate indefinite and definite integrals where a function's derivative appears in the integrand
  • Identify integrands that require integration by parts and apply the technique to evaluate them
  • Recognize which integration technique is most appropriate for a given integral
  • Solve application problems that require these integration methods

This Week's Topics

Section 6.1: Substitution

We begin with the technique of u-substitution, which is the reverse of the chain rule. You'll learn to identify when this method is appropriate and how to apply it effectively. Key concepts include:

  • Recognizing when a function's derivative appears in an integral
  • Choosing an appropriate substitution
  • Adjusting the bounds for definite integrals

Section 6.2: Integration by Parts

Next, we'll explore integration by parts, which is derived from the product rule. This powerful technique allows us to integrate products of functions. You'll learn:

  • The formula \( \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du \)
  • Strategies for choosing u and dv effectively
  • Handling repeated applications of integration by parts
