Approximation Using Trapezoid Rule and Simpson's Rule

Question

Use the Trapezoid Rule and Simpson's Rule with \( n = 6 \) to approximate the integral:

\[ \int_{1}^{4} 4 \cos(x^2) \, dx \]

Solution

To approximate the integral using the Trapezoid Rule and Simpson's Rule, follow these steps:

Step 1: Determine \(\Delta x\)

The given interval \([a, b]\) is \([1, 4]\), and \( n = 6 \). The width of each subinterval is:

\[ \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} = \frac{4 - 1}{6} = 0.5 \]

a) Trapezoid Rule

Using the Trapezoid Rule:

\[ \int_{1}^{4} 4 \cos(x^2) \, dx \approx \frac{\Delta x}{2} \left[ y_1 + 2(y_2 + y_3 + y_4 + y_5 + y_6) + y_7 \right] \]

Substitute the values:

\[ \frac{0.5}{2} \left[2.16121 + 2(-2.51269 - 2.61457 + 3.9978 - 3.64452 + 3.80148) + (-3.83064)\right] \]

Simplify:

\[ \begin{align*} &= \frac{0.5}{2} \left[2.16121 + 2(-2.51269 - 2.61457 + 3.9978 - 3.64452 + 3.80148) + (-3.83064)\right] \\ &= \frac{0.5}{2} \left[2.16121 + 2(-2.9725) - 3.83064\right] \\ &= \frac{0.5}{2} \left[2.16121 + (-5.945) - 3.83064\right] \\ &= \frac{0.5}{2} (-7.61443) \\ &= 0.25 \times (-7.61443) \\ &= -1.90361 \end{align*} \]

Result for the Trapezoid Rule: \( \approx -1.90361 \)

b) Simpson's Rule

Using Simpson's Rule:

\[ \int_{1}^{4} 4 \cos(x^2) \, dx \approx \frac{\Delta x}{3} \left[ y_1 + 4y_2 + 2y_3 + 4y_4 + 2y_5 + 4y_6 + y_7 \right] \]

Substitute the values:

\[ \frac{0.5}{3} \left[2.16121 + 4(-2.51269) + 2(-2.61457) + 4(3.9978) + 2(-3.64452) + 4(3.80148) + (-3.83064)\right] \]

\[ \begin{align*} &= \frac{0.5}{3} \left[2.16121 - 10.05076 - 5.22914 + 15.9912 - 7.28904 + 15.20592 - 3.83064\right] \\ &= \frac{0.5}{3} (7.25875 + 15.9912 + 15.20592 -16.24894) \\ &= \frac{0.5}{3} (22.46587 -16.24894) \\ &= \frac{0.5}{3} (6.21693) \\ &= 0.1666667 \times 6.21693 \\ &= 1.03616 \end{align*} \]

Result for Simpson's Rule: \( \approx 1.03616 \)
