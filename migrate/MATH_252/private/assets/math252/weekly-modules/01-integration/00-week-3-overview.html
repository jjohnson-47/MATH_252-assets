This week we dive into Chapter 6: Techniques of Antidifferentiation, learning our first major integration methods beyond basic antiderivative rules. You'll master two fundamental techniques that handle a wide variety of integrals encountered in calculus and real-world applications.

The big picture: While the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus shows us how to evaluate definite integrals using antiderivatives, finding those antiderivatives requires specific techniques. This week introduces the two most essential methods.

  • Solve real applications: Apply these methods to problems involving area, volume, and motion

üìñ Topics Covered This Week

Section 6.1: Integration by Substitution

We begin with u-substitution, which reverses the Chain Rule. This is often the first technique to try when faced with a complex integral. Key concepts include:

  • Pattern recognition: Identifying when a function's derivative appears in an integral
  • Choosing substitutions: Strategic selection of $u = g(x)$ to simplify the integrand
  • Definite integral techniques: Changing bounds vs. converting back to original variables
  • Common substitution patterns: Exponential, trigonometric, and algebraic functions

Section 6.2: Integration by Parts

Next, we explore integration by parts, derived from the Product Rule. This powerful technique handles products of functions and includes:

  • The LIATE strategy: Systematic approach for choosing $u$ and $dv$
  • The formula: $\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$ and how to apply it effectively
  • Repeated applications: When integration by parts must be used multiple times
  • Special cases: Integrals involving logarithms, inverse trig functions, and polynomials with exponentials
