Deriving Integration by Parts

Integration by parts comes from the product rule. Let's see how:

The product rule states that:

\[ \frac{d}{dx}[uv] = u\frac{dv}{dx} + v\frac{du}{dx} \]

Rearranging this equation:

\[ u\frac{dv}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}[uv] - v\frac{du}{dx} \]

Integrating both sides:

\[ \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du \]

This is the integration by parts formula, which transforms one integral into (hopefully) a simpler one.

Choosing u and dv

When applying integration by parts, we need to carefully choose which part of the integrand will be u and which will be dv. A helpful mnemonic is "LIATE":

  • Logarithmic functions (like ln x)
  • Inverse trigonometric functions (like arctan x)
  • Algebraic functions (like x²)
  • Trigonometric functions (like sin x)
  • Exponential functions (like eˣ)

Choose u from the list working from top to bottom, and pair it with a function whose derivative (dv) will be simpler.

Example 6.2.1: Basic Integration by Parts

Let's evaluate:

\[ \int x\sin(x)\,dx \]

Following LIATE, we choose:

\[ u = x \quad \text{(algebraic)} \] \[ dv = \sin(x)\,dx \quad \text{(trigonometric)} \]

Then:

\[ du = dx \quad \text{and} \quad v = -\cos(x) \] \[ \int x\sin(x)\,dx = -x\cos(x) - \int (-\cos(x))\,dx \] \[ = -x\cos(x) + \int \cos(x)\,dx \] \[ = -x\cos(x) + \sin(x) + C \]

Mastering Integration by Parts:

  • LIATE is your guide: Choose u following Logarithmic, Inverse trig, Algebraic, Trigonometric, Exponential
  • Practice makes perfect: The more you recognize the patterns, the faster you'll choose u and dv
  • Multiple applications: Don't be surprised if you need to apply the technique several times
  • Circular integrals: When you get back to the original integral, solve algebraically
  • Check your work: Differentiate your answer to verify it matches the original integrand
