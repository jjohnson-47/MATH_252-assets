This Week's Topics

Section 5.3: Riemann Sums

We'll explore how to approximate the area under a curve using rectangles, leading to the formal definition of the definite integral. This connects the geometric interpretation of integration with its analytical definition through limits of Riemann sums: \[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i)\Delta x \]

Section 5.4: The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

This crucial theorem establishes the connection between differentiation and integration. We'll learn both parts of the FTC and see how they make computing definite integrals much easier: \[ \frac{d}{dx}\int_a^x f(t)\,dt = f(x) \]

Section 5.5: Numerical Integration

We'll learn practical methods for approximating definite integrals when exact antiderivatives are difficult or impossible to find, including the Trapezoidal Rule and Simpson's Rule.
