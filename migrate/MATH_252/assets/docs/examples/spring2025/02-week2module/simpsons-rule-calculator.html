Simpson's Rule Calculator

Access the Simpson's Rule Calculator

This interactive tool helps you calculate definite integral approximations using Simpson's Rule. The calculator employs a numerical integration technique that approximates the area under a curve by fitting parabolas through sets of three points.

The Mathematics Behind the Tool

Simpson's Rule approximates a definite integral by breaking the interval into \( n \) even subintervals and applying parabolic approximations over pairs of subintervals. The basic formula for Simpson's Rule over a single interval \( [x_0, x_2] \) is:

\[\int_{x_0}^{x_2} f(x)\, dx \approx \frac{x_2 - x_0}{6}\left( y_0 + 4y_1 + y_2 \right),\]

where:

  • \( y_0 = f(x_0) \)
  • \( y_1 = f(x_1) \)
  • \( y_2 = f(x_2) \)

This formula simplifies the integral under the parabola passing through the three points \( (x_0, y_0) \), \( (x_1, y_1) \), and \( (x_2, y_2) \). The derivation involves integrating the quadratic polynomial that fits these points, which leads to this simplified expression.

By applying this over each pair of subintervals and summing the results, we get the general Simpson's Rule:

\[ S_n = \frac{\Delta x}{3} \left( f(x_0) + 4 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1,\, \text{odd}} f(x_i) + 2 \sum_{i=2}^{n-2,\, \text{even}} f(x_i) + f(x_n) \right), \]

where \( n \) is even, \( \Delta x = \dfrac{b - a}{n} \), and \( x_i = a + i\Delta x \).

How to Use This Tool

  1. Click the link at the top of the page to open the calculator in Google Colab.
  2. If prompted, sign in to your Google account.
  3. In the Colab notebook, you may need to click "Open in Playground" or "Connect" to start using it.
  4. Enter your function in terms of \( x \) (e.g., x^2, sin(x), exp(-x)) in the designated input cell.
  5. Specify the lower and upper bounds of integration.
  6. Choose the number of subintervals (must be even; more intervals result in a better approximation).
  7. Run the cells to see both the numerical approximation and visual representation.
  8. Experiment with different functions and parameters to build intuition.

Note: The tool automatically graphs both the function and the parabolic approximations, helping you visualize how increasing the number of subintervals improves accuracy.
