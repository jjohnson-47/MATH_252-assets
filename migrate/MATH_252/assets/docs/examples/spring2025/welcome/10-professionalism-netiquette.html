Professionalism and Netiquette Guidelines

Core Principles
  • Treat all members of our learning community with respect and courtesy
  • Assume positive intent in discussions while maintaining professional boundaries
  • Remember that tone can be difficult to convey online - choose words carefully
  • Consider each interaction as practice for your future professional career
Communication Standards
  • Write clear, concise messages with proper spelling and grammar
  • Use a professional tone in all communications, including emails to instructors
  • Include relevant subject lines and course information in emails
  • Respond to messages within 24-48 hours during weekdays
  • Emoticons are acceptable when appropriate, but maintain professionalism
Discussion Participation
  • Stay focused on course-relevant topics and materials
  • Support assertions with evidence and proper citations when applicable
  • Respect diverse viewpoints while maintaining academic discourse
  • Use inclusive language that respects all members of the learning community
Academic Integrity
  • Maintain academic honesty in all online interactions and submissions
  • Respect intellectual property and copyright in shared materials
  • Use appropriate citations when referencing others' work
  • Collaborate only when explicitly permitted by assignment guidelines
  • Review and follow the UAA Academic Integrity Policy
  • Familiarize yourself with the UAA Student Code of Conduct

Remember: Our online classroom is an extension of the university environment. Conduct that would be inappropriate in a physical classroom is equally inappropriate online. Violations of these guidelines may result in disciplinary action according to university policies.
