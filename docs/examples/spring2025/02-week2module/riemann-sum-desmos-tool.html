A Riemann sum is a method for approximating the total area under a curve (the definite integral) by summing up areas of multiple rectangles. The approximation improves as the number of rectangles increases.

The Riemann sum for a function \( f(x) \) over the interval \( [a, b] \) is given by:

\[ S = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i^*) \Delta x \]

where:

  • \( n \) is the number of rectangles.
  • \( \Delta x = \dfrac{b - a}{n} \) is the width of each rectangle.
  • \( x_i^* \) is a sample point in the \( i \)-th subinterval \( [x_{i-1}, x_i] \).
